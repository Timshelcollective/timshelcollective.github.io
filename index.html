What We Do

Timshel a Hebrew word meaning the power of choices is a collective for digitally active women to discuss, learn and practice solidarity, active citizenship and exercise their political agency. Our objective is to help women navigate and exercise their political agency to the best of their ability and intent.

EDUCATE

Organising learning programmes to understand and learn more about everyday activism, solidarity actions, prevalent human rights movements across the world, women in politics etc.

ADVOCATE

Rally together for common feminist goals, curating community resource/support kits and researching more ways to strengthen our agency

MOTIVATE

Provide a common platform to share our stories of struggle, solidarity and political action. A common platform to take some quick actions.

Who We Are

Society has used a mix of stereotypes, sexism and patriarchy to keep women away from politics. How often we (women) are asked to -- better not venture or deliberate on politics or talk about it because, in the estimation of most, if not all, men, "politics is not their (women’s) cup of tea". Furthermore, having always been looked down upon and admonished for discussing change in the drawing-rooms of our house to boardrooms of our organisations, this cynicism and hopelessness to be able to do nothing has set deep in us, enough to make us forget the power we hold in our individual agency. But no more, as individuals we hold the most influential power in our vote, wallet and voice and at Timshel our vision is to help awaken, strengthen and organise this agency of women so we can shape the change we want to see. As they say, if not us, then who and everything in this galaxy is made up of tiny single units. The smallest is the mightiest.

Why TIMSHEL

Studies show that women take more than 70% of consumption-related decisions, be it food, clothing, furniture or cars in families. They tend to be in charge of the health of their families and continue to be a major influence on both the education and activities of family members. Then why not political decisions? Across South Asia, women’s parliamentary representation range from a meagre 5.8 percent to a paltry 30 percent, still less than the minimum 33% equitable balance. Countless examples around the world, have demonstrated that feminist leaders and policymakers have heralded new ground-breaking work that focuses on benefitting the most vulnerable in society. This includes bettering the quality and consistency of public service delivery and creating more equal and caring societies.

With each passing day more and more women gaining economic freedom, having better access to information, avenues of participation and mobilisation itself— is a testament to the fact that we are at a tipping point of liberation, a moment we must hold onto. Since October 2017, more than 1 million women worldwide have shared experiences and solidarity with their sisters under the banner of #MeToo platforms. In 2017, the #LahuKaLagaan hashtag took over on Twitter to campaign against the 12 percent tax on sanitary napkins, with the “period tax” being scrapped in 2018 as a result of the movement. Pertinently Women are collectivising under common banners of solidarity and finding new ways to amplify their voice, vote and wallet.

How Do We Support Our Community?

Timshel faciltates its community to realise the tacit agency available within us to influence politics and effect a change and then to explore various avenues to express this agency. Through our platform we facilitate direct action-based steps (financial, vocal, allying etc.) for our community to actively support and participate the causes one believes in.

Amplifying Medium

Research and campaigns understanding, promoting civic participation of women
Individual inspiration stories
Platform to find allies and local community

Learning Source

Self realisation triggering Socratic reading discussions
Awareness and sensitisation Training
Educational and Critical Thinking Content
Listicles on various steps one can take
Individual inspiration stories

Solidarity Shop (Financial Actions)

One stop destination to make donations, purchase products which support a particular cause, pledge commitments etc.
